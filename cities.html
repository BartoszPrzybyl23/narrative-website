3. Zadar

Where is Zadar?

Zadar sits by the sea on the Adriatic coast. It's in the northern part of a place called Dalmatia, known for its beautiful coastline in southeastern Europe. Picture it kind of in the middle, between other Croatian cities like Split and Rijeka, enjoying the seaside vibes!


What to do in Zadar?

Explore the fascinating Old Town of Zadar, home to the famous St. Donatus Church and Roman ruins, to gain a deeper understanding of the past. See the captivating Sun Salutation light show at sunset, and take in the unique symphony of the Sea Organ, where the waves create soothing music along the waterfront. Visit museums like the Museum of Ancient Glass or the Museum of Contemporary Art to fully immerse yourself in art and culture. Don't pass up the opportunity to relax on stunning beaches like Kolovare or Borik and indulge in mouthwatering Croatian food at neighbourhood eateries. Zadar is a varied destination that appeals to a wide range of interests. Take into account going on a boat tour to neighbouring islands for beautiful views, or explore the lively nightlife scene with busy bars, quaint cafes, and live music venues.

2. Split

Where is split?

Split is situated southwest of Zagreb, the capital of Croatia. Located on the Dalmatian coast, it is a significant urban centre that perfectly combines modern culture, ancient history, and breathtaking views of the Mediterranean. In addition to its historical sites, the city has a vibrant waterfront promenade where both locals and tourists can experience the laid-back Mediterranean way of life, as well as quaint cafes and bustling markets. Split is an easy starting point for exploring the surrounding islands and other coastal locations along the Adriatic coastline because it is also a transportation hub.


What to do in split?

Explore the historic wonders of Diocletian's Palace in Split, where historical tales can be found around every corner. Scale the Saint Domnius Cathedral for expansive views and take in the interior's fascinating design. Take in the vibrant atmosphere and the views of the sea as you stroll along the bustling Riva Promenade. Don't miss Marjan Hill, which offers a tranquil haven for those who love the outdoors. Visit the museums to learn more about the city's history and take in cultural events at Peristyle Square. Visit Bacvice Beach to unwind and take in the sun and Adriatic Sea. Finally, if you want to see more of Croatia's breathtaking coastline in a single day, think about island hopping.

1. Dubrovnik

Where is Dubrovnik

Split is a dynamic city in the heart of the Dalmatian region. It is well-known for its rich history, which is exhibited by the historic Diocletian's Palace, and it offers a seductive fusion of historical sites, breathtaking coastal scenery, and a lively Mediterranean atmosphere.


What to do in Dubrovnik

While in Dubrovnik, immerse yourself in history by strolling along the majestic City Walls, offering breathtaking views of the Old Town and the sparkling Adriatic Sea. Explore the enchanting Stradun, the bustling main street, and visit landmarks like Dubrovnik Cathedral and the Rector's Palace to delve into the city's rich heritage. Wander through Sponza Palace and discover its historical treasures before unwinding at Banje Beach for a relaxing afternoon by the azure waters. For an adventure, hop on a ferry to Lokrum Island to wander through lush gardens and uncover a medieval monastery. Cap off your day by ascending Mount Srd for panoramic views or indulging in a Game of Thrones tour to discover filming locations. Dubrovnik offers a tapestry of historical wonders, scenic beauty, and cultural experiences, ensuring an unforgettable visit.
