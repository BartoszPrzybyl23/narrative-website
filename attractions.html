Attractions

Explore Croatia's diverse cultural attractions

Must see attractions!

Walls of Dubrovnik

The Mediaeval Walls of Dubrovnik once protected the "Pearl of the Adriatic" from sieges in the past. Currently a well-liked tourist destination, climbers get amazing views of the sea and Dubrovnik from these imposing stone walls. Known for serving as a filming site for television series such as Game of Thrones, the walls provide an insight into the past while maintaining a touch of movie charm.

Plitvice Lakes

Attractive enough to be named a European natural treasure, the Plitvice Lakes became a national park in 1949. With sixteen linked lakes that display vivid shades of azure, green, blue, and grey, the region is home to a wide variety of wildlife, some of which are endangered, such as the European brown bear, wolf, eagle, owl, and lynx.

Dinamo Zagreb

Tourists have the exciting chance to experience the thrill of Croatian football at Dinamo Zagreb, one of the country's top football teams. This legendary team, which was founded in 1945 and has a devoted following, ensures that events at Maksimir Stadium will be remembered forever.

Pula Arena

One of the six largest Roman amphitheatres still standing, Pula Arena is a remarkable example of ancient architecture. Constructed during the 1st century AD, this remarkable historical site could hold more than 26,000 spectators. Take in a live gladiator fight or explore the underground gallery to see preserved gladiatorial gear and weapons. This exquisitely restored arena offers guests a fusion of entertainment from the past and the present with world-class concerts featuring well-known performers like Sting and Elton John.

Krka National Park

It's not difficult to find amazing views in this, one of Croatia's most popular and well-liked national parks. Immersion in the breathtaking network of 17 waterfalls that cascade into emerald pools is the main draw for many visitors to Krka National Park. For those who want to take in the revitalising views of nature, others like to walk the park's trails.

Sea Organ

A masterpiece of viral content is the Sea Organ in Zadar. This architectural sound piece, which is now a permanent attraction in Zadar, was originally intended to be an experimental musical instrument. The Sea Organ creates natural melody as waves crash against it and the water seeps into the tubes beneath the marble stairs. Both length and size vary among the organ pipes, also known as tubes. As different waves enter different-sized organ pipes and make different sounds, these pipes continuously change the sounds produced by the waves.

Zlatni Rat

Travellers come to Zlatni Rat for vacations from all around the world. While many people just take pleasure in lounging in the sun and swimming in the seas, which change colour from turquoise to dark blue just ten metres off the coast, others may be spotted taking leisurely strolls in the century-old pine forest to escape the heat. In order to revive and reenergize for the day's activities, there is also a cafe!

Kornati Islands

The largest and densestly populated archipelago in the Adriatic, the Kornati Islands consist of 140 deserted islands, reefs, and islets, of which 89 are part of the Kornati National Park. The largest island, Kornat, is the source of the park's name. Diverse activities including swimming, hiking, fishing, diving, snorkelling, and animal viewing are all included in a visit. Among the famous sites are the mediaeval Church of Our Lady of Tarac, the historic Byzantine Fortress Tureta, and the memorial Dry Stone Walls honouring the firefighters who died in the Kornati tragedy.

Diocletian's Palace

Originally constructed as an imperial residence in the fourth century AD, the palace ultimately included a military castle and town. It is a breathtaking example of a well-preserved Roman-era building that you shouldn't miss when visiting Croatia. The mysterious palace was built by Diocletian using marble brought from Greece and Italy and 12 sphinxes and columns from Egypt. The historic palace is now a free-to-visit complex with stores, restaurants, and apartments, while certain structures may charge an admission fee.
